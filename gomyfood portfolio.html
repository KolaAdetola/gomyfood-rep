MAPU TOFU
Mapo tofu is a classic Chinese dish originating from Sichuan province. It's a flavorful and spicy dish consisting of tofu cubes cooked in a sauce made with fermented black beans, chili paste, garlic, ginger, and Sichuan peppercorns. The dish is typically garnished with minced meat, often pork or beef, along with chopped scallions or leeks.

White Truffles
White truffles are highly prized fungi with a distinctive aroma and delicate flavor. They have a rough, warty exterior and a marbled white interior. Found primarily in the Piedmont region of Italy, they grow underground in a symbiotic relationship with certain trees. White truffles are used sparingly in gourmet cuisine, shaved thinly over dishes like pasta, risotto, or scrambled eggs to impart their unique aroma and flavor. Due to their rarity and high demand, they are considered a luxury ingredient and are sold at premium prices.

Kobe beef
Kobe beef is a type of high-quality beef that comes from Wagyu cattle raised in the Hyogo Prefecture of Japan, with Kobe being its capital. Known for its exceptional marbling, tenderness, and rich flavor, Kobe beef is highly sought after by food enthusiasts worldwide. The meat is prized for its melt-in-your-mouth texture and intense umami taste. Kobe beef cattle are raised under strict regulations, including a specialized diet and rigorous breeding standards, resulting in meat of unparalleled quality. Due to its limited availability and meticulous production process, Kobe beef commands a premium price in the culinary world.
